How we choose our picks

We don’t just grab the newest model off the shelf. Instead, we comb through long-term owner forums, check what breaks over months of use, and read every credible expert review we can find. We look for bikes that are light enough for toddlers to manage on their own, with seats that adjust low so feet are flat on the ground. We also consider value, durability, and ease of assembly. A bike might look great in the box but be frustrating to maintain. Our recommendations are the ones that consistently earn praise for doing the basics right: smooth ride, solid tires (foam or pneumatic), and no sharp edges.
